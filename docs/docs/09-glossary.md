# Atmos Glossary

## Product terms

**Atmospheric Situation:** The interpreted summary of the current state, drivers and next meaningful transition.

**Living Scene:** The data-driven 2.5D environmental canvas on Home.

**Selected Time:** The single shared timestamp driving the scene, interpretation, metrics and charts.

**Insight:** A prioritized, evidence-backed explanation or useful timing window.

**Explore Mode:** Visual, explanatory interaction intended for broad accessibility.

**Analysis Mode:** Denser technical view with profile charts and source details.

**Provenance:** Metadata describing the source, kind, issue/observation time, validity and retrieval time of data.

## Meteorological terms

**CAPE:** Convective Available Potential Energy, an indicator of potential buoyant energy for convection. It does not by itself guarantee storms.

**Cloud base:** Approximate altitude of the lowest cloud layer; data source and method must be identified.

**Dew point:** Temperature to which air must cool for saturation at constant pressure; a useful moisture measure.

**Freezing level:** Altitude where the temperature profile crosses 0°C; may cross more than once in complex profiles.

**Geopotential height:** Height of a pressure surface adjusted for gravity; preferred for mapping pressure levels to approximate altitude.

**HRRR:** NOAA High-Resolution Rapid Refresh model, a convection-allowing model for the United States.

**Instability:** Atmospheric tendency to support vertical motion; represented through multiple parameters, not one definitive score.

**Pressure level:** Surface of constant atmospheric pressure such as 850 hPa. Its altitude varies.

**Pressure tendency:** Change in pressure over time.

**Relative humidity:** Moisture amount relative to saturation at the current temperature.

**Vertical velocity:** Model variable describing upward/downward motion, with sign conventions varying by source.

**Wind direction:** Meteorological direction from which the wind originates.

## Engineering terms

**Adapter:** Boundary converting a provider-specific response into Atmos domain data.

**Fixture:** Deterministic synthetic dataset used for development and testing.

**GRIB2:** Binary format commonly used for gridded meteorological model data.

**PWA:** Progressive Web App with installable/offline capabilities.

**Quality tier:** High, medium or low visual rendering mode selected for performance.

**Snapshot:** Normalized atmospheric state valid for one time and location.
